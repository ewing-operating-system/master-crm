Buyer Narrative

Grundfos is a Danish multinational and one of the world's largest pump manufacturers, with annual revenue exceeding €4 billion and operations in over 55 countries. Under CEO Poul Due Jensen's founding philosophy of sustainable water technology, the company has aggressively expanded beyond core pump manufacturing into full-spectrum water treatment solutions — a strategy that accelerated dramatically with the 2025 acquisition of Newterra, which gave Grundfos a $350M+ US water treatment platform virtually overnight. Inge Delobelle, based in Brookshire, TX, leads elements of Grundfos's North American commercial strategy.

What makes Grundfos a credible acquirer is not just their financial capacity — they are privately held by the Poul Due Jensen Foundation, meaning they can take a long-term view without quarterly earnings pressure — but their demonstrated commitment to building a US water treatment services business through acquisition. The Newterra deal signaled that Grundfos is willing to pay meaningful multiples for established platforms with service revenue, recurring customer relationships, and geographic density. They are not tire-kickers; they are executing a stated strategy with capital already deployed.

For a seller evaluating Grundfos, the key consideration is continuity. Grundfos has historically preserved the operational identity of acquired businesses while providing access to global R&D, supply chain, and brand credibility. Employees, customers, and vendor relationships tend to be maintained post-close, which matters for owners who care about legacy.

Why This Seller

AquaScience fills a precise geographic gap in Grundfos's post-Newterra US water treatment map. Newterra's strength is concentrated in the Midwest and Mid-Atlantic; New England remains underserved. AquaScience has built meaningful density across Rhode Island and surrounding states in both residential and commercial water treatment — exactly the local service relationships that a global manufacturer cannot replicate organically. The eCommerce channel is a bonus differentiator: it gives Grundfos a direct-to-consumer distribution model they can study and potentially scale across their broader US platform. At an estimated $8M in revenue, AquaScience is a manageable tuck-in that wouldn't require board-level approval complexity, yet it delivers a new territory and a new channel in a single transaction.
