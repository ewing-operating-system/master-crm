Buyer Narrative

Commercial Metals Company (CMC) is a publicly traded, Fortune 500 steel and building materials manufacturer headquartered in Irving, Texas, with over $8 billion in annual revenue. CMC has operated for more than a century, evolving from a scrap metal dealer into one of North America's largest vertically integrated steel producers, with operations spanning steel minimills, fabrication, and increasingly, precast concrete infrastructure products.

In recent years, CMC has made a decisive strategic pivot into precast concrete through a series of high-profile acquisitions. Their $675 million acquisition of CP&P and subsequent acquisition of Foley Products in 2025 signaled a clear thesis: CMC intends to build the dominant precast concrete platform in the United States, leveraging their existing distribution infrastructure, customer relationships, and balance sheet to consolidate a fragmented industry.

As a buyer, CMC brings institutional credibility, deep pockets, and a genuine operational understanding of heavy building materials. They are not a financial buyer looking to strip costs — they are an industrial operator assembling a national platform. For a seller, this means your business would join an organization that understands manufacturing, values operational excellence, and has the capital to invest in growth rather than simply extract returns. Peter Matt, as CEO, has been the architect of this platform strategy and is directly accountable for its execution.

Why This Seller

Design Precast & Pipe's Gulfport, Mississippi operation sits in the exact geographic gap between CMC's Southeast plants (bolstered by CP&P and Foley) and their Texas home base. CMC has systematically assembled precast coverage across the Mid-Atlantic and Southeast, but the Gulf Coast corridor — Mississippi, Louisiana, Alabama — remains an uncovered zone. Design Precast's established manufacturing capabilities in precast concrete and pipe, combined with their Gulf Coast distribution reach, would give CMC contiguous coverage from Texas through the Southeast without the time and cost of a greenfield buildout. At an estimated $15M in revenue, this is a bolt-on acquisition that CMC can absorb cleanly into their existing platform, and Pat Fore III's decades of operational knowledge would be a valuable asset during integration.
