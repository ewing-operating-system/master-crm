Buyer Profile

Culligan International is the world's largest consumer water treatment company, founded in 1936 and currently backed by Advent International (with BDT & MSD Partners as co-investors). Headquartered in Rosemont, Illinois, Culligan has transformed from a legacy water softener brand into a global platform spanning residential, commercial, and industrial water treatment across 90+ countries. Since Advent's acquisition in 2017, Culligan has executed over 200 tuck-in acquisitions — an extraordinary pace that signals both deep capital reserves and a well-oiled integration playbook. Their 2022 merger with Waterlogic, a $4.5 billion transaction, cemented their position as the undisputed global leader in consumer water services. Culligan's acquisition thesis is straightforward and battle-tested: acquire established local water treatment operators with loyal customer bases and skilled technicians, then layer in Culligan's national brand, product portfolio, supply chain purchasing power, and operational infrastructure. They pay fair multiples for quality businesses because they know exactly how to extract synergies — route density optimization, cross-selling filtration and softening products into acquired customer bases, and converting one-time equipment sales into recurring service revenue streams. Their corporate development team, led by CEO Scott Curl, evaluates hundreds of opportunities annually and has refined a diligence-to-close process that is among the fastest and most predictable in the industry. For a seller evaluating Culligan, the signal is clear: this is not a financial buyer looking to cut costs and flip. Culligan keeps local teams intact, retains customer relationships, and invests in growth. They have closed hundreds of deals, which means a smooth diligence process, certainty of close, and a buyer who won't get cold feet. Sellers typically stay on through a transition period, employees are retained, and the local operation gains access to Culligan's national brand recognition, marketing resources, and product catalog — making it a genuine 'best of both worlds' outcome for founders who care about their legacy.

Why AquaScience

AquaScience checks every box in Culligan's tuck-in acquisition playbook. A 41-year operating history in Rhode Island means an entrenched customer base with deep local trust — exactly the kind of goodwill that Culligan cannot replicate organically. The business serves both residential and commercial segments, giving Culligan immediate cross-sell opportunities across their full product line including softeners, filtration systems, reverse osmosis units, and bottle-free coolers. Rhode Island and the broader Southern New England corridor represent one of the highest-density, highest-value water treatment markets in the country due to aging municipal infrastructure, elevated PFAS and radon contamination concerns, and growing consumer awareness of water quality. At an estimated $8M in revenue, AquaScience is sized perfectly for Culligan's tuck-in model — large enough to be strategically meaningful, small enough to integrate without organizational disruption. AquaScience's 100,000+ customer relationships represent a captive base for Culligan's recurring revenue model, and their PFAS and radon remediation specialty positions them in the fastest-growing segment of residential water treatment. For Culligan, this is a Northeast density play that immediately fills a geographic gap while adding specialty capabilities in contaminants that are driving regulatory action and homeowner demand across New England.
