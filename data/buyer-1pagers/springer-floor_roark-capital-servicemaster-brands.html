Buyer Profile

Roark Capital Group is an Atlanta-based private equity firm founded in 2001 by Neal Aronson, managing approximately $37 billion in assets under management across its fund family. Roark specializes in franchise and multi-location business models, with a portfolio that includes Inspire Brands (Arby's, Dunkin', Buffalo Wild Wings, Sonic), CKE Restaurants, and ServiceMaster Brands. Their thesis is built around acquiring market-leading franchise platforms in fragmented industries, then using operational discipline, technology investment, and tuck-in acquisitions to drive same-store growth and geographic density. They are patient capital — Roark holds investments longer than typical PE, often 7-10 years, because their returns come from compounding operational improvements rather than financial engineering. In 2020, Roark acquired ServiceMaster Brands from Terminix parent ServiceMaster Global Holdings for approximately $1.5 billion (the broader ServiceMaster enterprise has been valued at $5.5B+ across various transactions). ServiceMaster Brands encompasses ServiceMaster Clean, ServiceMaster Restore, Merry Maids, and other franchise cleaning and restoration concepts operating through approximately 4,800 franchise locations across North America. Post-acquisition, Roark has invested heavily in franchisee support systems, technology platforms for scheduling and routing, and national account programs that drive volume to local operators. Their stated goal is to make ServiceMaster Brands the dominant platform in every subcategory of residential and commercial cleaning services. Roark's playbook at ServiceMaster Brands follows their broader strategy: acquire the platform, professionalize operations, then aggressively fill geographic white space through both organic franchisee development and strategic tuck-in acquisitions of independent operators. They have been particularly focused on converting independent cleaning and floor care businesses into ServiceMaster franchise locations or acquiring them outright to operate as company-owned units in markets where franchise recruitment has been slow. The Midwest represents a key expansion corridor where ServiceMaster Brands has historically been under-penetrated relative to coastal markets.

Why Springer Floor

Springer Floor fits the ServiceMaster Brands acquisition strategy on three critical dimensions. Geographically, Des Moines represents exactly the kind of mid-market Midwest metro where ServiceMaster Brands needs density. Iowa is a state where their franchise footprint is thin relative to population — they have ServiceMaster Clean franchisees but lack the floor care specialization that Springer brings. Acquiring an established operator with 30+ years of customer relationships, commercial contracts, and local brand equity is dramatically faster and cheaper than recruiting a new franchisee or building from scratch. Springer's estimated $900K revenue base, while modest, represents a proven book of recurring commercial and residential floor care business that can be immediately scaled using ServiceMaster's national account relationships, marketing infrastructure, and cross-selling with Merry Maids and ServiceMaster Restore customers in the region. Operationally, Springer Floor under Terri Holman has built exactly the kind of business Roark looks for in tuck-ins: owner-operated, profitable at a sub-$1M scale, with deep local knowledge and customer loyalty that would take years to replicate. Roark's ServiceMaster platform can layer technology (CRM, routing optimization, online booking), purchasing power (equipment, chemicals, insurance), and national account revenue on top of Springer's existing base. The financial profile is also attractive — at $900K revenue, this is small enough that it won't require board-level approval or extensive diligence, fitting into the category of bolt-on acquisitions that a platform's M&A team can process efficiently. Floor care is a natural adjacency to ServiceMaster Clean's existing janitorial and carpet cleaning services, making integration straightforward with minimal operational risk.

We'd like to understand how ServiceMaster thinks about geographic expansion in Iowa and whether owner-operated floor care businesses fit their acquisition criteria. Instead, frame it as a selective conversation about how ServiceMaster Brands is building density in the Midwest and whether there might be mutual interest in exploring a relationship with a well-established Des Moines floor care operator. If the conversation progresses, introduce Springer Floor as a specific opportunity where the owner may be open to exploring strategic options with the right partner who can preserve the business's reputation while providing growth resources.

Risk Factors
  • Size concern — at $900K revenue, Springer may fall below ServiceMaster Brands' minimum acquisition threshold. Many PE-backed platforms set floors at $1-2M revenue for tuck-ins to justify transaction costs, legal fees, and integration overhead.
  • Owner dependency — if Terri Holman IS the business (key customer relationships, daily operations management), ServiceMaster may worry about post-acquisition revenue attrition without a management team in place to ensure continuity.
  • Franchise model conflict — ServiceMaster Brands primarily grows through franchising. They may prefer to recruit a new franchisee in Des Moines rather than acquire an independent operator, especially if the economics of a franchise fee plus ongoing royalties exceed the returns on an outright acquisition.
  • Market size limitation — Des Moines metro population of roughly 700K may not support the growth trajectory ServiceMaster needs from a company-owned location, raising questions about whether the addressable market justifies the acquisition versus organic franchise development.
Expected Timeline

PE-backed platform tuck-in acquisitions at this size typically move faster than platform-level deals but still require corporate process. From first meaningful conversation to an indication of interest (IOI) is usually 3-5 weeks, as the corporate development team evaluates strategic fit and requests basic financial information. If interest is confirmed, a formal LOI typically follows within 2-4 weeks after receiving a confidential information memorandum. Due diligence for a sub-$1M revenue acquisition is streamlined — usually 30-45 days focused on customer concentration, contract transferability, and employee/equipment assessment rather than extensive financial audits. Total timeline from first contact to close is typically 90-120 days, though ServiceMaster's corporate structure could add 2-3 weeks for internal approvals. The key accelerant is getting to the right person quickly — if this routes through Roark's fund team first, add 3-4 weeks of internal redirection before substantive conversations begin.
