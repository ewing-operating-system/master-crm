Buyer Narrative

Roark Capital Group is one of the largest and most operationally focused private equity firms in the franchise and multi-unit service space, managing over $37 billion in assets. Founded by Neal Aronson in Atlanta in 2001, Roark has built its reputation by acquiring franchise-driven businesses and systematically scaling them through disciplined operations, brand investment, and geographic expansion. Their portfolio reads like a who's-who of American service and restaurant brands — from Inspire Brands (Arby's, Buffalo Wild Wings, Dunkin') to ServiceMaster, CKE Restaurants, and dozens more.

In 2020, Roark acquired ServiceMaster Brands in a deal valued at approximately $5.5 billion, carving out the franchise services division from what was then ServiceMaster Global Holdings. That acquisition gave Roark control of ServiceMaster Clean, Merry Maids, ServiceMaster Restore, and Amerispec — a portfolio of cleaning, restoration, and home inspection brands with over 5,000 franchise locations. The thesis is straightforward: fragmented residential and commercial cleaning services represent a massive addressable market where brand trust, operational consistency, and local density drive outsized returns.

For a seller evaluating Roark as a potential acquirer, the key differentiator is operational seriousness. This is not a financial buyer looking to strip costs and flip — Roark builds brands over long hold periods, invests in the people and systems that make local operations work, and has a proven track record of integrating tuck-in acquisitions without disrupting what made the acquired business successful in the first place. They understand owner-operators because their entire model depends on them.

Why This Seller

Springer Floor operates in exactly the kind of market that ServiceMaster Brands targets for geographic infill — a mid-sized Midwestern metro where the company currently has limited floor care density. Des Moines is a stable, growing market with strong commercial and residential demand for floor care services, and Springer has already done the hard work of building local brand recognition, establishing recurring customer relationships, and developing the operational infrastructure that ServiceMaster would otherwise need to build from scratch.
