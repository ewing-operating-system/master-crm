Buyer Narrative

BDT & MSD Partners is a merchant bank formed from the 2023 merger of Byron Trott's BDT Capital Partners and Michael Dell's MSD Partners, managing over $40 billion in assets. Byron Trott — famously called 'the only banker I trust' by Warren Buffett — built his reputation advising ultra-high-net-worth families and founder-led businesses on generational wealth decisions. The firm specializes in helping family-owned and founder-operated companies find strategic paths forward, whether that's growth capital, succession planning, or full exits. They are not financial engineers looking to strip assets; they are relationship-driven investors who understand what it means to build something from scratch.

Their acquisition of Culligan International in 2023 signaled a major thesis: essential home services with recurring revenue are generational assets worth consolidating. Under BDT & MSD's ownership, Culligan has accelerated its tuck-in acquisition strategy, rolling up regional water treatment providers to fill geographic gaps and add capabilities. The playbook is proven — acquire strong local operators, keep the team in place, layer on Culligan's national brand and supply chain, and let the business compound. For a seller, this is the rare buyer who has both the capital to close quickly and the operational platform to actually grow what you've built.

Byron Trott personally understands founder psychology. He has spent three decades sitting across the table from people who built businesses with their own hands and are wrestling with what comes next. That empathy translates into deal structures and transition plans that respect the seller's legacy — not just their EBITDA. When BDT & MSD acquires through Culligan, sellers are joining a platform that values operational excellence and customer relationships, not one that's going to gut the team and rebrand overnight.

Why This Seller

AquaScience checks every box in Culligan's tuck-in acquisition criteria. Geographically, Culligan's national footprint has a documented gap in the Northeast corridor — Rhode Island and southern New England represent whitespace that AquaScience already owns. At approximately $8M in revenue, the company is the right size for a tuck-in: large enough to be meaningful, small enough to integrate without disruption. What makes AquaScience particularly compelling is the differentiated eCommerce channel. Culligan's growth has been built on traditional residential and commercial service routes; AquaScience brings a proven digital acquisition engine and fulfillment operation that Culligan could replicate across its entire national platform. Larry Casey has built a business with sticky recurring revenue, loyal customers, and operational discipline — exactly the kind of foundation Culligan wants to plug into its supply chain, brand, and back-office infrastructure. This isn't a turnaround; it's an accelerant.
