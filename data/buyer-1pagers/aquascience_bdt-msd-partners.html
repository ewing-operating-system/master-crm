Buyer Profile

BDT & MSD Partners is a merchant bank formed through the 2023 combination of Byron Trott's BDT & Company and MSD Partners (the investment firm of Michael Dell). Headquartered in Chicago, the firm manages over $40 billion in assets and serves as both a strategic advisory platform and a principal investor alongside the world's wealthiest families and founders. Byron Trott, the firm's Chairman & CEO, built his reputation at Goldman Sachs as Warren Buffett's preferred banker — known for discretion, long-term relationship investing, and a deep network of ultra-high-net-worth families and founder-operators. BDT's core thesis centers on backing market-leading businesses in essential, recession-resistant sectors where fragmented ownership creates roll-up economics. The crown jewel of BDT's portfolio in the water and environmental services space is Culligan International, which BDT acquired control of through its fund vehicles. Under BDT's ownership, Culligan has become one of the most aggressive acquirers in the residential and commercial water treatment industry, executing dozens of tuck-in acquisitions across North America and Europe to build density, expand service territories, and consolidate a historically fragmented market. Culligan's playbook is straightforward: acquire well-run local and regional water treatment businesses, retain the existing management and customer relationships, layer on Culligan's national brand recognition, purchasing power, and operational infrastructure (routing software, fleet management, water testing labs), and drive margin expansion through scale. BDT provides Culligan with patient capital and a long hold horizon — they are not flipping the business in 3-5 years but building a generational platform. BDT's operating philosophy aligns closely with founder-friendly transactions. They do not pursue hostile deals. They do not bring in outside management teams to replace founders on day one. Their pitch to sellers is continuity — your name may change, but your people, your customers, and your community presence remain. This matters enormously when approaching owner-operators like Larry Casey who have spent decades building a business and care about legacy. BDT's network of family-office co-investors also means they can move quickly and creatively on deal structure, offering sellers flexibility on earnouts, rollover equity, and transition timelines that traditional PE firms often cannot match.

Why AquaScience

AquaScience represents a textbook Culligan tuck-in target. The company operates in the Northeast — Rhode Island and surrounding New England markets — where Culligan has historically had thinner coverage compared to the Midwest and Southeast. AquaScience's 41-year operating history, 100,000+ customer base, and specialization in PFAS and radon remediation give Culligan immediate density in a high-value geography where water quality concerns (aging infrastructure, PFAS contamination from military bases and industrial sites) are driving sustained demand growth. The dual-channel model — traditional service routes plus eCommerce — is particularly compelling because Culligan has been investing heavily in digital customer acquisition and direct-to-consumer sales channels. AquaScience's proven eCommerce capability is an operational asset Culligan could study and replicate across its national footprint. Financially, at an estimated $8 million in revenue, AquaScience sits squarely in Culligan's tuck-in sweet spot — large enough to move the needle in the New England territory, small enough that the transaction can be executed quickly without extensive regulatory review or complex integration. Larry Casey's decades of ownership and the company's established reputation mean Culligan would be acquiring a stable, recurring-revenue business with deep community roots and minimal customer churn risk. The PFAS and radon specialization is a strategic premium — these are the fastest-growing segments in residential water treatment, driven by tightening EPA regulations and increasing consumer awareness. Culligan has been building out its remediation capabilities nationally, and acquiring a recognized specialist like AquaScience accelerates that buildout in a region where PFAS contamination is a front-page issue.

This language resonates with BDT's own positioning as the 'founder-friendly' firm and creates alignment from the first touchpoint.
