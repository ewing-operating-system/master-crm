Buyer Narrative

BDT & MSD Partners is a merchant bank founded by Byron Trott, one of the most respected dealmakers in American finance. Trott built his reputation as Warren Buffett's preferred banker at Goldman Sachs, advising on landmark transactions including Berkshire Hathaway's $6.5 billion investment in Goldman during the 2008 financial crisis. In 2009, he founded BDT & Company to advise and invest alongside family-owned and founder-led businesses — the kind of owners who built something real and want a partner who respects that legacy. The firm merged with MSD Partners (Michael Dell's investment office) in 2023, creating a combined platform with over $40 billion in assets under management.

Their investment thesis is built around long-term partnerships with exceptional businesses, not financial engineering. They don't flip companies. They buy platforms they believe in and build them over decades. Culligan International is the crown jewel of this approach — they acquired it and have systematically executed a tuck-in strategy, rolling up regional water treatment businesses across North America to expand Culligan's national footprint. Every acquisition strengthens the platform's density, service capability, and brand reach.

For a seller evaluating BDT & MSD, the signal is clear: this is a buyer that understands owner-operated businesses, pays fair prices, and keeps the teams that built the company. Byron Trott didn't build his career by squeezing founders — he built it by earning their trust. That's why Buffett, the Pritzker family, and dozens of other principal owners have chosen to work with him repeatedly.

Why This Seller

AquaScience fits the Culligan tuck-in playbook almost perfectly. AquaScience brings an $8M revenue base with an established residential and commercial customer book, which gives Culligan an immediate operating presence in the region without a cold start. This isn't just a geographic tuck-in; it's a capability acquisition. Culligan gets a proven digital playbook they can replicate across their entire platform, and AquaScience's customers get access to Culligan's national brand, supply chain, and service infrastructure.
